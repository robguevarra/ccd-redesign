# Open Questions for the Pitch Meeting

**Authored:** 2026-05-05
**Purpose:** Flip the pitch from monologue to conversation. Bring this list. Ask 3–5 of these in the first 10 minutes — before walking through the demo — to make Dr. Hsu feel heard. Each question is grounded in something the audit surfaced; the answers shape v2 priorities.

---

## Brand & voice

1. **The site says "dentisthsu.com"; your Yelp page says "Comfort Care Dental." Which name is the practice's identity going forward?** *(Surfaced in audit §10 — there's a real brand-identity split today. The new build needs one canonical name.)*

2. **When you look at TMJ Expert, what's the *specific* moment that you wished was on your own site?** *(Lets him point at the reference rather than describe it abstractly. His answer becomes the design north star for the wow zone.)*

3. **Your blog has both practice technology posts and personal/family stories. How do you want patients to experience that mix?** *(Surfaced in audit §2 — content quality finding #3. Three real options: (a) keep them mixed, (b) separate the family content into its own namespace, (c) move the family content to a different domain. His answer is irreversible-ish, so we ask now.)*

## Services & current offerings

4. **Of the 12+ services on your current site, which two do you want patients to *actually* think of you for?** *(The current site treats all services with equal weight. Picking the two anchors lets us promote them in IA and dedicate hero treatment.)*

5. **You've written substantively about your CBCT scanner adoption (2014, 2020) and 3Shape Trios upgrade (2024). Are there other technology investments you'd want patients to know about?** *(The technology-adoption story is genuinely differentiating; surfacing it is leverage.)*

6. **Are there services on your site you'd actually like to retire or de-emphasize?** *(Many sites have stale service pages from years past. This question gives him permission to declutter.)*

## Patients & differentiation

7. **Describe the patient who walks out of your office happiest. What did they come in for?** *(Forces a story, not a category. Reveals positioning. The answer informs hero copy.)*

8. **Who's the patient you wish you had more of? Demographics, situation, or service-need?** *(Audit can't tell us this; the dentist can. Answer informs SEO targeting and content priorities.)*

9. **What's a question you get from new patients that the current site doesn't answer well?** *(Drives FAQ content + on-page copy priorities.)*

## Photography & assets

10. **Would you be open to a 2–3 hour photo session at the office post-signing?** *(Per master spec §5 photography direction option E — his actual portrait + clinic interiors are the credibility anchor. Answer determines whether v1 ships with editorial-stock-only photography or with his real photography baked in.)*

11. **Is there a photo or moment from the practice — from any era — you'd want preserved into the new site?** *(Sometimes there's a single image or family memento that matters more than the dentist will say unprompted.)*

## Logistics & v2 expectations

12. **You're using Weave + OpenDental for practice management. If we do a v2, would you be open to letting us integrate the new site with those — or do you prefer to keep them separate?** *(Audit didn't surface this; it's from your earlier brief. But it's a real v2 fork worth nailing down before scoping the engagement.)*

13. **What's your timeline tolerance for v2 launch after the pitch — 6 weeks, 12 weeks, longer?** *(Calibrates v2 scope. Master spec §3 phases assume 6+ weeks; if he wants faster, we cut scope; if longer, we add depth.)*

14. **Beyond the dental and family blog content, is there anything *not* currently on the site that you'd want to be?** *(Open-ended catch — sometimes reveals a side of the practice the site doesn't show today.)*

---

> **Pitch-day usage:** ask 3–5 of these (not all 14). Best openers: #2, #4, #7, #10. Save #12 and #13 for the close, framed as "if we move forward together…"
